Core Technology

UFLY AI

The AI Pilot: Deep Reinforcement Learning

Our AI doesn't follow pre-programmed waypoints. It learns to fly from scratch using PPO, mastering the full 4D control space in a custom physics engine.

🧠 Custom Physics Engine

We built a custom aerodynamic simulation with non-linear CL-α stall modeling for the NACA 4412 airfoil — so the AI learns realistic stall recovery and flight dynamics at all attitudes.

🎮 4D Continuous Control

Ailerons, Elevator, Rudder, Throttle — all simultaneously controlled by the neural network with action low-pass filters (α=0.18) preventing actuator hard-overs.

📡 20-Dimension Observation Space

Virtual sensor suite: IMU gyro (100Hz), Pitot tube (20Hz), Barometric altimeter, GPS (5Hz), target direction vector. Same inputs as certified avionics.

3-Phase Curriculum Learning

Phase 1Stabilization

200K steps — Wings-level flight, attitude hold, basic survival.

Phase 2Navigation

22-episode skill blocks, progressive turns from 20° to 60° banked coordinated turns.

Phase 3Mission Execution

Multi-waypoint routes, wind disturbances, domain randomization for sim-to-real transfer.

Phase 4Full Autonomy

Ground takeoff → cruise → waypoint navigation → approach → landing. Complete mission cycle.

How It Works

UFLY AI

Autonomous Mission Profile

A typical UFLY AI cargo delivery — fully autonomous from engine start to shutdown.

🛫

Takeoff

AI manages throttle ramp-up, rotation at V_R, and initial climb to 2,400m cruise altitude.

📈

Climb

Constant speed climb at V_Y. AI balances pitch and throttle for optimal climb rate.

✈️

Cruise

198 km/h at 8,000 ft. AI navigates multi-waypoint routes with coordinated turns up to 60°.

📉

Approach

Descent planning, speed reduction, flap deployment. AI adapts to crosswind and gusts.

🛬

Landing

Precision touchdown with AI-managed flare, rollout deceleration, and engine shutdown.

Key: At every phase, the PPO neural network continuously controls all four axes (aileron, elevator, rudder, throttle) at 100 Hz — reacting to wind gusts, turbulence, and sensor noise in real-time. No human intervention required.
